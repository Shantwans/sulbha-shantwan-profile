# ABOUT PAGE

## My Story

I did not begin my journey trying to become an expert.

I began as a teacher trying to solve a problem.

How do we help learners truly connect with what they are learning?

Over the years, that question became bigger.

I noticed that many students remembered facts but forgot experiences.

Teachers were overwhelmed.

Parents were confused by rapidly changing expectations.

Schools were preparing children for a future that was changing faster than ever before.

So I started experimenting.

I designed games.

I created stories.

I built interdisciplinary learning experiences.

I trained teachers.

I led innovation projects.

I explored new technologies.

I became a homeschool parent.

I returned to research.

Each experience added another piece to the puzzle.

Today, my work sits at the intersection of learning, creativity, human development, and educational innovation.

---

## What I Believe

### Learning should be experienced, not delivered.

People rarely remember information.

They remember experiences.

The best learning experiences create emotion, curiosity, challenge, reflection, and connection.

---

### Technology should amplify humanity.

Technology is powerful.

But education is ultimately about people.

The goal is not to create smarter machines.

The goal is to help humans become more thoughtful, creative, ethical, and capable.

---

### Stories are among our oldest learning technologies.

Before there were textbooks, there were stories.

Stories help us remember, connect, imagine, and understand.

That is why storytelling continues to influence much of my work.

---

### Curiosity is more powerful than compliance.

Real learning begins when questions matter more than answers.

Sometimes those experiences take the form of stories.

Stories help learners enter ideas before they study them.

## Games

Games create safe spaces for exploration.
